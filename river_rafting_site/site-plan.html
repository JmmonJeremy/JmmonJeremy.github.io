Overview

Purpose

Our goal is to expand our business and to increase our company's profits. In order to do this, we will be clearly informing the public of the available services and products that we offer. We will entice the public to use our services by also portraying the happiness and satisfaction that will result from creating a personal adventure using our services. We want to portray the value of the experience that a customer will have. We want to present a clear perception that we are a quality company that provides an excellent value for the consumer's investment in our services and products.

Audience

There are three main target audiences we will be focusing on. The first target group is families (specifically the parents - because they will be making the decision and paying the bill). The families we will be focusing on will have teenagers within the family who are looking to have a fun time or an adventure in the outdoors. The parents will be looking for a fun family experience that is exciting and yet safe enough for the whole family. They will also want the experience to help bring their family closer together. This focus group will most likely access our site on their phone, but they could also do so on a laptop or desktop computer. The second target audience consists of social groups (such as church groups, club associations, and camping groups). This audience will be made up of people ranging in age from approximately twelve to sixty years of age. The main need we will be trying to fill is providing an activity that will be a fun experience that can deepen relationships among the group. This focus group will also most likely access our site on their phone. The final target audience will be businesses groups seeking to build team unity. This group will be made up of adults between the ages of twenty to sixty years of age. They will be seeking a safe and satisfying activity that can be used to teach principles of teamwork. This target group will most likely access our site on a laptop or desktop computer.

Typography

Heading Font: Permanent Marker

I chose this font because its rounded letters give a water like look to the letters and also give the appearance of movement like rapids. I thought this would be a fun font for headlines and would also be a good contrast from the paragraph font.

Paragraph Font: Open Sans

I chose Open Sans for the paragraph font because it is very readable. I also thought it was close enough to the Courier New (monospace) that was listed as a safe font for the web by WCSchools.org. I also think this font adds a professional look to the type.

The Site Map of a site is just like it sounds…it is a map of the pages in a site and how they are related and linked together. From the map above we can see that we will eventually have the Home page and 2 sub or child pages.

The lines that connect them all together indicate that each page should be accessible from any other page, it is essentially showing us the global navigation for the site.

Wireframes

Wireframes are like blueprints for making webpages. They should show the major sections of content that will be on the page and the relative locations of each element. In the wireframe below you can see there will be 6 sections to our page:

Home Page's Wireframe

  1. At the top we have a section with the logo (the box with the mountain means an image) and the navigation bar.
  2. Then there is a banner image that stretches all the way across the screen.
  3. Next we have some text and an image
  4. ...followed by another row made up of an image and some text.
  5. Then one more section of text with no image.
  6. Lastly, a footer containing a copyright/name line and 3 social media icons.

River Routes Page's Wireframe

  1. At the top the header is the same layout as the Home page header.
  2. Then there is a headline for the first river route followed by some text and a medium sized image to the right of the text.
  3. Next we have a large image with a call to action button that has text to the right of it.
  4. Underneath that there is text with another medium sized image to the right of the text again.
  5. Then there is another headline for the second river route that has text and images in the same pattern as the first river route layout.
  6. There is one more headline with text and images for the third river route that follows the same pattern as the first river route layout.
  7. Lastly,the footer is the same layout as the Home page footer.

Blog Page's Wireframe

  1. At the top the header is the same layout as the Home page header and River Routes header.
  2. Then there is a small image followed by a headline with some text underneath it and a call to action button to the right of it.
  3. The next section has two small images with a headline and text between them.
  4. Underneath that there is a subheadline with a bunch of text under it and a large picture to the right of it.
  5. Below that is a second subheadline that follows that same pattern with the text and large image.
  6. The last section is three medium sized pictures next to each other with a headline and text under them.
  7. The text is followed by one large banner image stretching across the bottom of the page with a call to action button on it.
  8. Lastly,the footer is the same layout as the Home page footer and the River Routes footer.
